Examples of Our Recent Research

Examples of our recent research across South Asia, demonstrating our approach to generating policy-relevant evidence.

Women's Self-Help Groups Study

A mixed-methods study examining how participation in government-supported SHGs affects women's financial autonomy, household decision-making, and community leadership across rural and peri-urban areas.

Research Question: "How do different models of SHG support (training, mentorship, credit linkages) affect women's economic empowerment outcomes, and what role do social norms play in mediating these effects?"

Climate Migration Research

Participatory research with climate migrants in Chittagong's informal settlements, using photovoice and community mapping to understand barriers to healthcare, education, and social protection services.

Research Question: "What institutional barriers prevent climate migrants from accessing urban services, and how do community networks and informal institutions fill these gaps?"

Post-Conflict Education Study

Longitudinal study tracking educational outcomes and inter-ethnic relationships among youth in post-conflict areas, combining administrative data with ethnographic research methods.

Research Question: "How do different models of school integration affect learning outcomes and social cohesion in post-conflict communities, and what role do teachers and curricula play?"

Water Governance Analysis

Policy analysis and ethnographic study of women's participation in water user associations, examining how gender norms and institutional design affect water governance outcomes.

Research Question: "Under what conditions do women's water user committees improve both water management and women's political participation in rural communities?"

Healthcare Access for Displaced Populations

Ethical research protocol developed with local youth groups to understand healthcare barriers among internally displaced populations, focusing on trauma-informed and culturally sensitive approaches.

Research Question: "How do ongoing security concerns and displacement affect healthcare-seeking behavior, and what community-based interventions can improve access to mental health services?"

Digital Financial Services Impact

Randomized controlled trial evaluating the impact of mobile money training for garment workers, with particular attention to remittance patterns and household financial management.

Research Question: "Does training in digital financial services increase savings rates and remittance efficiency among internal migrants, and how do these effects vary by gender and origin location?"
