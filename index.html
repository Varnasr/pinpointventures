Our Research Philosophy

Three core principles guide our approach to development research and evaluation across South Asian contexts.

Community-Centered Design

Research questions, methodologies, and tools are co-developed with communities. We prioritize participatory approaches that ensure research serves those most affected by development policies and programs.

Methodological Rigor

We combine quantitative and qualitative methods to capture both breadth and depth. Our mixed-methods approach reveals patterns while preserving the complexity of lived experiences.

Policy Relevance

Every research project is designed to inform policy and practice. We translate findings into actionable recommendations that can drive systemic change in development outcomes.

Research in Action

Examples of our recent research across South Asia, demonstrating our approach to generating policy-relevant evidence.

West Bengal, India

Women's Self-Help Groups and Financial Inclusion

A mixed-methods study examining how participation in government-supported SHGs affects women's financial autonomy, household decision-making, and community leadership across rural and peri-urban areas.

Key Research Question

"How do different models of SHG support (training, mentorship, credit linkages) affect women's economic empowerment outcomes, and what role do social norms play in mediating these effects?"

Chittagong, Bangladesh

Climate Migration and Urban Service Access

Participatory research with climate migrants in Chittagong's informal settlements, using photovoice and community mapping to understand barriers to healthcare, education, and social protection services.

Key Research Question

"What institutional barriers prevent climate migrants from accessing urban services, and how do community networks and informal institutions fill these gaps?"

Batticaloa, Sri Lanka

Post-Conflict Education and Social Cohesion

Longitudinal study tracking educational outcomes and inter-ethnic relationships among youth in post-conflict areas, combining administrative data with ethnographic research methods.

Key Research Question

"How do different models of school integration affect learning outcomes and social cohesion in post-conflict communities, and what role do teachers and curricula play?"

Sindh, Pakistan

Water Governance and Women's Leadership

Policy analysis and ethnographic study of women's participation in water user associations, examining how gender norms and institutional design affect water governance outcomes.

Key Research Question

"Under what conditions do women's water user committees improve both water management and women's political participation in rural communities?"

Manipur, India

Healthcare Access in Conflict-Affected Areas

Ethical research protocol developed with local youth groups to understand healthcare barriers among internally displaced populations, focusing on trauma-informed and culturally sensitive approaches.

Key Research Question

"How do ongoing security concerns and displacement affect healthcare-seeking behavior, and what community-based interventions can improve access to mental health services?"

Dhaka, Bangladesh

Digital Financial Services and Labor Migration

Randomized controlled trial evaluating the impact of mobile money training for garment workers, with particular attention to remittance patterns and household financial management.

Key Research Question

"Does training in digital financial services increase savings rates and remittance efficiency among internal migrants, and how do these effects vary by gender and origin location?"
